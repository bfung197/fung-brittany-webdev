Are Koalas facing extinction?

If I were to pick an animal I most relate to, I would choose the koala. It sleeps for 16 hours a day, and although I cannot say the same for myself, I certainly would like to. Among the countless interesting facts about koalas, perhaps the most astonishing is that koala populations have been decreasing rapidly. The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Services have listed the koala on the threatened list, due to severe habitat loss. Some are even arguing that the koalas' status should be changed from threatened to endangered because their numbers have dropped so low.

There is still a lot of debate over whether or not the koala should be on the endangered list. This debate dates back to over five years ago. In April 2012, the Australian government voted that the koala be considered "vulnerable". However, the Australian Koala Foundation does not agree and maintains that the koala is "critically endangered".
